#LDB

Rules for the Little Drummer Boy Game

A holiday game wherein you lose when you realize that you’ve heard any version of The Little Drummer Boy.

Thanksgiving-December 26 12:01 AM



Rules


  1. You may not force others to lose the game

    If someone plays a version of LDB for an in-person audience for the express purpose of ruining someone's game, the player has not lost the game.

  2. Mass-Media Broadcasts are fair game

    Playing the LDB song via a mass-audience medium (e.g. a radio station) is considered fair game.

  3. Anyone has power to call you out

    If a player is exposed to the LDB song, but has not (yet) recognized it, anyone may call the player's attention to the song while it is playing.

  4. You may listen to the song if new 1 time and 1 time only

    If you are new to the LDB game, you may purposely listen to the song here one time and one time only.

  5. Don't be a sore loser

    It is considered 'Good Form' to announce any losses to fellow players. (This especially applies to non-binding losses during the 'Pre-Season'.)

'Tis The Season'

The Game begins on the fourth Thursday of November. In the U.S., this is known as Thanksgiving. If you are not celebrating Thanksgiving with a meal on this day, then your game starts at 12:01AM. If you are having a Thanksgiving meal on this day, then your game starts as soon as you finish your Thanksgiving meal.

The ‘Regular Season’ lasts until 12:01AM on December 26th. If a player hasn’t been knocked out during the ‘Regular Season’, they may opt to continue playing in the ‘Bonus Round’ which lasts until 12:01AM on January 1st. Before the 2011 season, this was the stated ending rule: “The game is played until midnight on New Year’s Eve. If you have not heard the LDB song by that time, you win!”

History of the LDB Game

After publishing this site for the 2010 Holiday Season, I was contacted by several people who claimed to have created the LDB Game around 2000-2001, and wrote down the rules for their Facebook Group in 2009.

A few of their suggestions have been incorporated for the 2011 and later seasons, but keep in mind that other LDB players may be playing from a different ruleset.
